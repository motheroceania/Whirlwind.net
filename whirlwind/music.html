☆ favorite artists

Björk

probably one of my top favorite artists of all time, Björk not only moves me by her music, but she also inspires me. with the way she spans across almost every genre of music in her own way, to how distinct she is with her visuals and style.

i discovered her around 2018, my brother was a pretty big fan of her, and one day played on of her songs "Crystalline" which interested me quickly. her Debut album might've secured my love for her though.

Thom Yorke

my recent favorite of this list, i like to think Thom is one of the biggest influental figures when it comes to alternative music.

obviously i discovered him through Radiohead, but i feel like some often don't decide to look into his solo work and other projects. as like he is in Radiohead, his art carries something special to me, like leaves lasting effects on you till today.

Portishead

ive only started listening to Portishead recently, maybe around like april or may of this year. and let me tell you they are probably one of my top favorites of now. they not only popularized the Trip-hop genre (even if they aren't font of that title), but also helped me get into it.

theres something about how their music pulled me in. their mash up of fused hip-hop production and old spy film samples, along with the lead singer Beth's haunting vocals made me fall instantly in love with their work.

Michael Jackson

my most favorite ever since i was in elementary. Michael is an american singer, songwriter, dancer, and philanthropist - that fundamentally influenced pop culture.

i discovered him roughly through my family since i was little. (they dont play about him) and my god does his discography never fail to move me. i feel like everytime i decide to listen to his music, it always feels fresh and relevant.

Radiohead

my top favorite british people ever (sigh), Radiohead has stuck with me since around 2018ish - 2019 and has never left my head. i mean what DO i say about them? their music captures me in a way i can't explain.

you might think i discovered them from "Creep", but it was mostly typical "The Bends" with their songs "High and Dry", "Street Spirit" and my favorite, "Black star". it was around 2023 is where i picked them up again and became heavily autistic about them (sorry to anyone i annoyed).

Gorillaz

my favorite virtual artists of all time, Gorillaz was one of those bands that stuck with me during my childhood era of music. they not only inspired me to pick up more instruments because of Damon Albarn, but also with digital art with their distinct style thanks to Jamie Hewlett.

i discovered them around my elementary years or so through their self titled debut album and hearing "Feel Good Inc." through an old youtube video. then picked them up again in middle school when i heard "DARE". their style and lore behind them pulled me in HARD though.

☆ minilog

8/18/2024 - ...okay, i love pablo honey... maybe because i was slightly brainwashed from the general side of radiohead fans saying its "radiohead's punching bag" and " ugh its SOOO not like the OMEGA Ok Computer and the rest of their works...", but after i gave it a lot of listens - especially the Drill EP, i come to find that its actually a pretty good album. not 100% with their best works, but a good grunge/alt album. i always love a band's first album since thats where they birthed and started learning. people seem to forget that learning is a key to create greatness.

still dont like creep though, i really can't bring myself to like it im sorry :(. it starts to feel like its a cover more than a song Thom actually wrote.
