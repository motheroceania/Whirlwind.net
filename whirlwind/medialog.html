dancer in the dark

★★★★★
released: 17 May 2000
genre: Musical, Psychological

my all time favorite that i'll never watch again

click for review

i was going to write something lengthy, but i figured i should save it for a blog post that analyzes it piece by piece someday, since this is really something that came, took a chunk of my heart, and left with it.

one of the most painful films, and a thoughtful way of examining how music and cinema as a means of escapism. i felt absolute physical pain watching this, paired with the soundtrack that i think perfectly encapsulates visualizing yourself in something you love just to cope with the inevitable.

Björk's performace moved me so much that i cried like a child. it was just too cruel to take her character's innocence and naivety, just to torture me as i grip my head and look away multiple times. its like she LIVED through her character. and her artistry in the music she produced for the film still sticks with me. it might even be my favorite movie soundtrack of all time.

my first time watching it was months ago, but i can still memorize everything that happened. god i hope Lars Von Trier goes to hell.
